LandLedger

A blockchain-based property registration system that represents land titles as ERC-721 tokens, secured by a "Two-Gate" model — a 3-of-3 multi-signature quorum plus a biometric identity hash — with document storage handled off-chain via IPFS. Built as my final-year prototype.

View Source Code

> the_problem

Land registries in many places are paper-based, centralized, and vulnerable to fraud: records can be forged, altered, or lost, and a single corrupt official can transfer a title illegitimately. LandLedger asks what a registry looks like if ownership is an immutable on-chain record and no single party can move a title alone.

> key_features

  • ERC-721 land titles: each property is a non-fungible token, giving every parcel a unique, transferable, tamper-evident on-chain identity.
  • Two-Gate security: a transfer requires both a 3-of-3 multi-signature quorum (e.g. seller, buyer, and registry authority) and a matching biometric identity hash, so no single actor can move a title.
  • IPFS off-chain storage: heavy documents (deeds, surveys) live on IPFS with only their content hash stored on-chain, keeping the ledger cheap while preserving integrity.
  • Fraud-blocking tests & gas benchmarks: a functional test suite covers valid transfers and blocked fraud attempts, plus a script measuring exact gas cost and a real-world agency-cost comparison.

> technologies_used

Solidity Hardhat ERC-721 IPFS React Node.js

> what_i_learned

Building LandLedger taught me to think about security as a protocol, not a feature — designing the multi-signature quorum and biometric gate forced me to reason about who can do what, and what happens when one party is compromised. I also learned the practical economics of blockchain: what belongs on-chain versus off-chain, and why gas cost is a first-class design constraint rather than an afterthought.
